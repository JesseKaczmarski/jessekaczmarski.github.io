About me


I am a 4th year Ph.D. candidate at the The University of New Mexico's Department of Economics. My research focuses on industrial organization and electricity economics in the Western United States under the lens of ongoing grid modernization. Prior to UNM, I received a Bachelor of Arts in Economics and a Master of Science in Resource and Applied Economics from the University of Alaska Fairbanks. I have extensive experience in contingent valuation research and more recently have begun using high-frequency data to examine economic questions related to power plant operations, energy mix management, and more recently, the role of utility-scale storage in the electric grid. By training, I am an applied microeconomist with a focus on econometric approaches to complex systems.

At UNM, I am a long-term research assistant with the New Mexico Smart Grid Center. My primary research focuses on the use of high-frequency smart meter data coupled with original surveys to assess the role of household characteristics in predicting load profiles and profile attributes.

Research Interests
